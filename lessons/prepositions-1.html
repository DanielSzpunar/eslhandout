Prepositions: Part 1

  • Home
  • Lessons
  • Prepositions: Part 1

Difficulty Level: ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆


Prepositions are not essential to communicating in English. However, Prepositions are the key to becoming fluent.

You can have a vast set of vocabulary to choose from, but if your prepositions are missing or you're using the wrong ones, your English will never be impressive!


When to use "to":

  • a. To indicate a place that you will go or direction you will move towards.
  • b. To indicate an ending point or a limit.
  • c. To indicate a time or period.

a. "I'm going to China next week."

a. "Can you please line up to the right."

b. "Apple stock dropped to $94.70 after closing."

b. "You can drive up to 100 km/h on the highway."

c. "I lived in Korea from 2012 to 2016."

c. "She works from 9 to 5 during weekdays."


When to use "for":

  • a. To indicate what something is used for.
  • b. To indicate why (because of).
  • c. To indicate duration or time.

a. This lense is for my camera.

a. We usually use the dance hall for weddings on the weekends.

b. We are sorry for your loss. (Because of your loss we are sorry.)

b. For this reason, I've decided to move. (Because of this reason, I've decided to move.)

c. I lived here for only a year.

c. He was the president for two terms.


When to use "of":

  • a. To indicate a reference to something.
  • b. To indicate an amount or number.
  • c. To indicate a connection or belonging to something.

a. I moved to South Korea in the summer of 2012.

a. This is a picture of my family.

b. A large number of South Koreans speak English.

b. I usually eat a bucket of chicken on the weekends.

c. I always dreamed of becoming a millionaire.

c. The best part of the book is in chapter one.
