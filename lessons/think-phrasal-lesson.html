Phrasal Verbs Using Think

  • Home
  • Lessons
  • Phrasal Verbs Using Think

Difficulty Level: ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆


There are several very common useful phrasal verbs in the English language using the verb "think". Let's take a look!


Think about / Think of:

These are the two most common prepositions that follow "think". "Think of" is when we think of something for a brief moment or very short time. "Think of" is also really useful for opinions.

"Think about" on the other hand is when we think about something for a slightly longer time; something we are considering. If it's too confusing to understand the subtly don't worry and just use them interchangebly.

1.) "What do you think of my new shoes?"

2.) "Everytime I saw a Starbucks when I lived in Korea, I thought of my hometown, Seattle."

3.) "Everytime I hear our song, I think of you."

4.) I need to starting thinking about my future.

5.) I spent the weekend thinking about moving to another city.

1.) What do you think of when you hear the song "Gangnam Style"?

2.) Have you ever though about living in a foreign country?

3.) Have you ever thought about switching career paths?

Use the phrasal verb "think of / think about" in your answer to the above three questions.
