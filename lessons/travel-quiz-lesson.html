What's The Question: Travel

  • Home
  • Lessons
  • What's The Question: Travel

Difficulty Level: ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆


Many ESL students spend countless hours answering questions in English for IELTS, OPIC, TOEIC, and TOEFL tests. However, very few students take the time to learn how to properly ask questions in English.

This quiz will make you feel more confident when asking questions in English while traveling. Good Luck!


Quiz:

Below you will see a statement. Ask yourself what would the question be and write it down. Make sure to use full sentences.

Some correct sample answers are provided at the bottom of the quiz. However, remember there are more than one way of asking these questions and remember to always be polite (excuse me / please / thank you / etc...)



  • 1.) The washroom is located at the end of the hall on your right-hand side.
  • 2.) Buses M7, M20, and M42 go to Times Square.
  • 3.) The bus fare is $2.75.
  • 4.) It takes about 30 minutes to get to Times Square from here.
  • 5.) I'm sorry, we don't have any small sizes available at the moment.
  • 6.) I'm sorry, unfortunately we are all sold out of white sweatshirts.
  • 7.) The total is $47.50.
  • 8.) Actually there is a delicious steak house located two blocks, north from here.
  • 9.) I'll have a sirloin steak with a baked potato, please.
  • 10.) I'll have a coke to drink, please.
  • 11.) Of course. I'll be right back with the dessert menu.
  • 12.) Our hotel check out time is at 11:30 a.m.
  • 13.) The pool opens at 9:00 am and closes at 9:00 pm every day.
  • 14.) We will send some fresh towels to room 203 as soon as possible.
  • 15.) The hotel shuttle bus leaves to the airport every two hours, starting at 6:00 am.
  • 16.) Flight 352 begins boarding at 12:10 pm.



Daily Expressions, Phrasal Verbs & Idioms:

1.) "Help yourself": This phrase means to not hesitate when taking something. If someone tells you to "help yourself" it means you can have as much as you want.

"Please help yourself to our hotel's continental breakfast, served from 9:00 am until 10:00 am daily."

2.) "On the house": This means that something is being given to you free of charge. Restaurants in America often give customers drinks or desserts "on the house".

"We are sorry to have kept you waiting to be seated for so long. Please enjoy a round of beers on the house."
