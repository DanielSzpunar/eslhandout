Difficulty Level: ★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆


Complete the multiple-choice quiz by selecting the best-suited answer for the question.


Quiz:

  • 1.) I've been shopping around for a _______ to England, but everything is more than I can afford. (a.flight price b.good flight c.cheap flight d.discount airline)
  • 2.) With the ______ pound, now is a good time to travel to England to get the most bang for your buck! (a.week b.weak c.lower d.expensive)
  • 3.) My new job only allows for me to take one _____ _____ from work this year. (a.week away b.week off c. week out d.holiday vacation)
  • 4.) I've always dreamed of having my economy class tickets _______ to first class. (a.raised b.promoted c.upgraded d.increased)
  • 5.) My family loves package tours. However, I've never been ______ ____ them myself. (a.big with b.big on c.big at d.impressed with)
  • 6.) We didn't pay for ______ __ baggage, so everything we have is carry-on. (a.cargo b.check-in c.flight d.first-class)
  • 7.) It's always good to book your accommodations in __________. (a.person b.cash c.advance d.style)
  • 8.) Sometimes it's good to negotiate a ______ rate with taxi drivers to save money. (a.tourist b.flat c. good d.low)
  • 9.) Traveling ______ can be a life changing experience for some. (a.broke b.overseas c.abroad d.cheap)
  • 10.) It's always useful to learn a few ___ ______ to help you get by while in a foreign country. (a.difficult sentences b.strange expressions c.key phrases d.polite gestures)



Match:

Match the words from column 1 with the best-suited answer from column 2.


Column 1 Column 2
1.) "big on" a.) steep / bad deal
2.) in advance b.) costly
3.) flat rate c.) low-quality / someone who doesn't like to spend money.
4.) gesture d.) reasonably priced
5.) over-priced e.) a signal / sign
6.) expensive f.) living quarters / lodging
7.) cheap g.) enjoy / interested in
8.) inexpensive h.) a charge / fee that stays consistent.
9.) accommodations i.) beforehand



Questions:

1.) What are you really big on when traveling? Why?

2.) How far in advance do you usually book your vacations?

3.) Do taxis in your country offer flat rates?

4.) Is your significant other cheap? Had you ever brought it up?

5.) Do you talk with your hands? Do you use any gestures?

6.) Do you have you eye on any trips or travel dates?

7.) Is your country's currency strong or weak right now in oversea markets? What countries are currently good to travel to?

8.) What is the ideal length for an overseas vacation?

9.) How much are you budgeting for your next trip?

10.) What is something you experienced at home or abroad that took your breath away?


Daily Expressions, Phrasal Verbs & Idioms:

1.) "A tourist trap": A place where the locals take advantage of tourists with high prices and inferior souvenirs.

Insadong is South Koreas biggest tourist trap.
